# Vision Doc Format Guidelines

## Context
Strategic documents that set direction - product vision, strategy docs, roadmap narratives. Most polished of the work formats, but still not corporate fluff.

## Characteristics
- **Length**: Medium to long (2-5 pages typically)
- **Formality**: Professional, polished, but still direct
- **Structure**: Clear narrative arc with logical sections
- **Tone**: Confident, forward-looking, grounded in reality

## Specific Patterns

### Document Flow
- Starts with where we are / why this matters
- Builds to where we're going
- Ends with what we're doing about it
- No "executive summary" unless required - the doc should be readable

### Voice
- Proper grammar and capitalization throughout
- Longer paragraphs (4-6 sentences) when building narrative
- "We" when talking about team/company
- Direct statements about strategy: "This is where we're headed" not "We believe this could potentially"
- OK to be bold: "This will change how users interact with ads"

### Strategic Thinking
- Context matters - explain the market/competitive/technical landscape
- Make trade-offs explicit: "We're choosing X over Y because..."
- Show you've thought it through
- Data and examples support claims
- Acknowledge risks without dwelling on them

### Structure Elements
- Clear section headers
- Can use numbered lists for roadmap items or principles
- Tables work for comparisons or timelines
- Prose is primary, structure supports it

### Technical Content
- Less technical detail than PRD
- Focus on "what" and "why" more than "how"
- Technical concepts explained enough for cross-functional audience
- Keep precision but add context

### Future Tense
- Confident: "We will build" not "We plan to build"
- Specific when possible: "Q2 2026" not "in the near future"
- Realistic: don't promise what can't be delivered

## Examples

### Opening Section:
```
## Where We Are

The ad tech landscape shifted in the last 18 months. Third-party cookies are effectively dead, and the replacement solutions - contextual targeting, first-party data, privacy sandboxes - are all partial answers. No single approach gives advertisers the reach and precision they had before.

This creates an opening. Publishers who can provide clean, privacy-compliant audience data without cookies have leverage they haven't had in a decade. But the window is narrow - maybe 12-18 months before the market consolidates around a few dominant solutions.

We're well-positioned but not moving fast enough. We have the tech infrastructure and publisher relationships. What we're missing is a coherent story about how our approach wins in this new landscape.
```

### Vision Statement:
```
## Our Direction

We're building the infrastructure for privacy-first audience targeting. Not as a single product, but as a platform that publishers and advertisers can build on.

This means three things:

1. **First-party data activation** - Help publishers turn their logged-in users into targetable audiences without sharing PII. We handle the identity resolution, segmentation, and matching.

2. **Cross-publisher orchestration** - Enable audience reach across multiple publisher properties through privacy-preserving collaboration. Think of it as a clean room that works in real-time.

3. **Measurement without tracking** - Provide campaign analytics that work without user-level tracking. Aggregate insights that are actually useful, not just privacy theater.

We're not trying to recreate cookies. We're building something better - targetable reach that works within privacy constraints and actually deserves user trust.
```

### Roadmap Narrative:
```
## How We Get There

The build is sequenced in three phases:

**Phase 1 (Q1-Q2 2026): Foundation**
Ship the data clean room infrastructure and get three publishers live. Focus is on proving the technical approach works and that advertisers get results. Success metric: 10M monthly active users in the system with match rates above 60%.

**Phase 2 (Q3 2026): Scale**
Expand to 10+ publishers and launch the cross-publisher audience extension. This is where the network effects kick in - each new publisher makes everyone else's audiences more valuable. Target is 50M MAU and demonstrate reach that matches cookie-based targeting.

**Phase 3 (Q4 2026-Q1 2027): Platform**
Open the APIs for third-party apps and integrations. Let DSPs and data providers build on our infrastructure. At this point we're not just a vendor, we're the rails that privacy-first ad tech runs on.

Timeline is aggressive but achievable. The risk isn't technical - we know how to build this. The risk is market timing. If we're 6 months late, someone else owns this space.
```

## Transform Guidance

### From AI → Vision Doc
- Remove hedging and corporate safe language
- Make strategic claims direct and confident
- Keep paragraphs substantial but readable
- Ensure narrative flow between sections
- Use "we will" not "we plan to" or "we hope to"
- Cut throat-clearing and excessive setup
- Preserve polish but add conviction
- Make trade-offs and choices explicit

### From Draft → Polished Vision
- Strengthen weak claims with specifics
- Ensure logical flow and narrative arc
- Check that forward-looking statements are realistic
- Verify data and examples support the argument
- Polish grammar and structure while keeping voice
- Don't let it become corporate fluff
- Keep the confidence and directness
